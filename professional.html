Professional Life

Experience with Elon Student Television

I began my work with Elon Student Television (ESTV) my freshman year. I started off as a writer for Elon Tonight where I was writing sketch comedy skits similar to Saturday Night Live. Outside of doing writing, I also worked on the sets as a gaffer or camera operator.

I continued working on this through my freshman year until the end of the year when I was offered the position of Marketing Manager for all of ESTV. I have continued this position til today where I create graphics for social media to promote all four shows as well as advertise upcoming events. In September 2022, I was additionally offered the role of co-host on the show E-Talk where I'm given my own segment to talk movies.

Filmography

My first addition to my filmography here at Elon was my short film titled "On the Tracks". I wrote, directed, and produced this short film that deals firsthand with depression and the process of overcoming grief. The film went on to premiere at the Raleigh Film & Arts Festival where it was nominated for Best Student Short. I'm also working on a dark comedy titled "What Went Wrong?". Outside of my films, I have worked as a grip on the Cinelon film "Chores" as well as gaffer on a promotional trailer for Turner Theater.

Journalism / Film Criticism

I began my tenure as a film critic back in 2017 on Letterboxd where I review films. Since starting, I have reviewed over 900 films, averaging over 200 new films a year. My experience as a film critic has lent me more knowledge of this genre which in turned help me get a job at FandomWire. I started doing film journalism back in June 2022, where I worked for FandomWire as a video essay writer. I wrote essays on how to make a proper reboot, how She-Hulk is the most comic accurate show, and how "X" is a love letter to 70s horror films.
